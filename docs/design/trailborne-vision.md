# Trailborne — vision and positioning

> Brand-level framing for the Trailborne mod. This document answers *what is
> Trailborne about*, not *how it works*. Mechanics live in `design/nomap.md`,
> `specs/`, and `datasets/`. This document is the thesis.

---

## The thesis: the Explorer role

Vanilla Valheim hands you a perfect satellite map after twenty minutes of walking.

Trailborne takes that away — and gives you something better in return.

Trailborne reframes Valheim around an **Explorer role**. The world doesn't reveal
itself for free anymore. Maps become artifacts you make, signs become tools you
place, and one player in your group — the one who likes wandering, who notices
landmarks, who remembers which valley had the iron — becomes genuinely
*load-bearing* to the party.

Your fighters still fight. Your builders still build. Your farmers still farm.
But now your **trailblazer** carries something the rest of you can't: the map
you actually live inside.

---

## Deeper, not harder

This is not a hard-mode mod. It's a *deeper* mod.

The same world, the same bosses, the same loot — but you have to learn to *see*
it, and that seeing becomes its own gameplay loop, all the way from your first
deer to the Mistlands.

We are not adding pain. We are adding a place for a kind of attention Valheim
has never quite supported before.

---

## On any SBPR server running Trailborne

The map you press M to view is **mostly fog**. Walking around no longer reveals
terrain. The only way to *see* the world on your map is to draw it.

To draw it, someone has to **go look**.

That someone is your Explorer. They carry the Trailblazer's Spade. They mix
pigments and paint signs on the trails. They build beacons on high ground so
the fighters can find their way back. They wear the Seer's Stone and pin every
berry patch, every copper vein, every dungeon entrance they pass. They make the
maps the rest of your group reads.

If your group has no Explorer, you'll find your way by dead reckoning and
torchlight. That's fine — Valheim has always rewarded paying attention to the
sky. But you'll feel the absence of someone whose *job* it is to know where
things are.

If your group has an Explorer, they're the reason you never get lost.

## Public release and Niflheim

**Trailborne is a publicly-released, standalone Thunderstore mod.** Any server
that wants the Explorer role can install it.

Niflheim is **one** SBPR server that uses Trailborne (alongside other SBPR
mods). Niflheim is not Trailborne's only home. Trailborne is designed to stand
alone — install it on any Valheim server, vanilla or modded, and the Explorer
role becomes available.

The mod is server-gated in a slightly different sense: gameplay-altering patches
check whether the server has opted into Trailborne behavior before firing. This
protects players who install Trailborne and then join a vanilla server — the
mod is silently inert there, no conflicts. But Trailborne *itself* is for
everyone who wants this kind of game.

## Vocabulary anchors (for downstream docs)

These are the terms we use consistently across PLAYER_GUIDE, design docs, and
specs. When a doc deviates, this is the canonical source.

- **The Explorer** — the role; a player who specializes in trailblazing.
- **Trailborne** — the mod.
- **The Trailblazer's Spade** — the tool that lets the Explorer place signs, paths,
  lamps, and other navigation infrastructure. Peer of the Hammer and the Hoe.
- **The Explorer's Bench** — the Meadows-tier crafting station that gates the
  Trailborne progression.
- **Painted Signs** — the colored, text-bearing posts the Explorer places in the
  world. Each is also a map pin.
- **Cairns** — maintained stone landmarks placed by the Explorer; lifecycle
  rehearsal for the Guardian Stone mod family.
- **The Seer's Stone** — Mountains-tier accessory that reveals "wisps" over
  pickable clusters and locations within the wearer's sight range.

These are not negotiable per-doc. If a doc needs a different term, it goes here
first.
